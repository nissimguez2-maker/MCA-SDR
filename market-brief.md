# Pass 1 Reconciliation: Market Brief

Ranked by impact on funded deals under the constraints. This cuts.

## Top opportunities to pursue (ranked)

1. **Re-engage the existing funded or repaid book and aged company leads first, with a trigger hook.** [Investment, Outbound, Trend] Proven intent, lowest default, near-zero cost, fits the clawback window. Fastest funded deals; starts before anything is built.

2. **Build the qualification page as a hard-gating, transactional, consent-capturing warming surface.** [Trend, Search, Investment, Loan Officer, Outbound] Mirrors the merchant's urgent query language, captures TCPA one-to-one consent, runs a payback calculator, and screens out defaulters and bad-fit merchants before they reach call time.

3. **Make document collection a first-class engine: mandatory post-call request draft, 48-hour chase, funder-submission tracker.** [Loan Officer] Converts interest into funded files. Partial statements, expired offers, and uncleared stips are the biggest leaks, and nothing else owns them.

4. **Run UCC-1 near-payoff self-sourcing as the cold engine from week one.** [Outbound, Trend, Investment] Free to sub-100 USD, fresh, high-intent: a lien proves the merchant qualified, and 60-150 days post-filing signals re-up. Ends dependence on company-lead flow.

5. **Rank the call queue by profitability and clawback survivability, not just approvability.** [Investment, Loan Officer, Search] Encode the roughly 20x EV gap toward 18-plus month, 30-50k USD, no-stack merchants. Needs funded-date and clawback-window fields in Supabase.

## Parked opportunities (real, not now)

- **LinkedIn signal monitoring** [Outbound]: competes with UCC and calls for scarce hours; revisit if UCC flow runs thin.
- **Referral ask at funding** [Outbound]: high value but only pays once deals close; switch on from week 3.
- **Reddit and community**: zero funded deals in 90 days. Keep only as zero-cost pain-language harvesting for copy.
- **Targeting stacked or declined merchants broadly** [Trend]: highest default and clawback risk; pursue only the clean near-payoff subset inside items 1 and 4.
- **Compliant messaging as a standalone growth play** [Trend]: fold into the page and compliance baseline; table stakes, not an opportunity.

## Key market needs to address

- Fast, compliant document collection after a verbal yes.
- Transparent factor-rate-is-not-APR tools and honest "bank said no" answers in a market that distrusts brokers.
- A trigger and signal layer so re-engagement and sourcing are not blind cold calls.
- Screening for clawback survivability, not just approvability.
- Page copy in the merchant's own urgent words, with a hard gate for merchants in default.

## Conflicts

- Aged-lead vendors: Outbound treats them as a usable Tier 3 source; Investment calls them toxic, recycled to 5-10 brokers at 30-40 percent default.
- Stacked or declined re-engagement: Trend sees an underworked pool; Investment and Loan Officer say avoid the highest-default segment.
- Market size: Trend and Investment flag the plan's implied 18-22B USD as inflated by blended revenue-based financing and factoring.
