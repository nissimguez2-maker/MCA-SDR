# Pass 2 Audit: Discovery Coach Lane

**Lane:** Call structure and qualifying questions behind tiering, from opener through tier assignment to document ask.

**Strategy:** The MCA discovery call is not a consultative sale; it is a rapid file-quality screen with a warm close. Spend the first 8-10 minutes running a structured intake that surfaces approvability AND clawback survivability simultaneously. Map every answer to a tier decision in real time. If the merchant clears the floor on revenue, time, and position, the last 5 minutes convert the verbal yes into a live document request. The call should disqualify loudly and early, not softly and late. Silence during hard questions (NSF days, stacking) is a red flag, not a buying signal.

---

**Tactics**

**Tactic 1: Open with an upfront contract and a single anchor question (minutes 0-2)**

Set the agenda before asking anything financial. Merchants distrust brokers; naming the process reduces defensiveness.

Script:
> "I have about 10 minutes of questions, mostly about your business finances. At the end I will tell you honestly whether we can get an offer to you and how fast. If the numbers do not fit, I will say that too. Fair enough?"

Anchor question (opens the file-quality screen):
> "Walk me through roughly what your business deposits in a month, ballpark is fine."

A merchant who hedges, deflects, or gives a range that spans $8k-$40k is already flagging inconsistent deposit patterns. Note the hesitation; probe it in the revenue block.

**Tactic 2: Run the 5-question financial intake in order, do not skip or reorder (minutes 2-10)**

This is the core tiering instrument. Each question has a pass threshold and a disqualify trigger. Ask them in this sequence because each answer anchors the next.

Q1, Monthly deposits (revenue proxy):
> "What do your last 3 months of bank deposits average per month, total across all accounts?"

Pass: $15k+. Tier 2 floor: $10k-$15k. Disqualify trigger: under $10k OR "I use multiple accounts but I am not sure of the total" (fragmented statements will not fund).

Q2, Time in business:
> "How long has the business been open, month and year you started?"

Pass: 12+ months. Soft pass for Tier 2: 6-11 months if deposits are $25k+. Disqualify trigger: under 6 months regardless of revenue.

Q3, Existing MCA positions (stacking screen):
> "Do you have any current business loans, merchant cash advances, or daily or weekly payment obligations going out of your account right now?"

If yes: "How many, and roughly what are the combined daily or weekly payment amounts?"

Pass: none, or one advance with balance under 30% of monthly deposits. Tier 2: one advance, balance under 50%. Disqualify trigger: two or more active positions (stack), or daily debits that consume more than 40% of average daily balance. Do not soften this question. Stacking is the top clawback cause and must be caught here, not at underwriting.

Q4, Negative days and NSF history:
> "In the last 3 months, have there been any days where your bank balance went to zero or negative, or any returned items on the account?"

Pass: zero or one isolated incident with an explanation. Disqualify trigger: "a few times" or any acknowledgment of chronic negatives. Chronic negative days signal a cash-flow structure that cannot survive daily MCA debits, which is the definition of a clawback-risk file.

Q5, Average daily balance (ADB):
> "Off the top of your head, what does your balance usually sit at mid-month, not end of month, mid-month?"

This is a sanity check against the deposit number. A merchant claiming $40k monthly deposits but a $2k mid-month ADB has either heavy payroll, excessive obligations, or is inflating the deposit number. Flag the gap; it will show in the statements.

Tier decision after Q5:
- Tier 1 (call today, move fast): $25k+ deposits, 18+ months, no stack, ADB consistent, zero NSF days. These are also the highest-profitability, highest-clawback-survivability files (Opportunity 5). Treat them as first-call priority.
- Tier 2 (work this week): $15k-$25k deposits, 6-17 months, at most one clean advance. Worth submitting; watch the file.
- Tier 3 (nurture, page them): $10k-$15k deposits, approaching 6-month mark, one advance nearing payoff. Follow up in 30-60 days or when the existing advance is within 45 days of payoff.
- Pause (end call politely): under $10k, under 6 months, two or more active positions, chronic NSFs, or no bank account in the business name. Do not take the application.

**Tactic 3: Use of funds and urgency as a soft clawback signal (minutes 8-10)**

After the financial intake, ask:
> "What is the funding for specifically, and how fast do you need it?"

> "If you got funded in the next 2-3 business days, is that timing workable for you, or do you need it sooner?"

"Payroll on Friday" or "we are behind on rent" signals a merchant already in cash distress. That is a clawback-survivability flag, not a buying signal. High urgency plus high distress plus an existing advance is the profile that defaults inside the clawback window. Log it; do not kill the call, but weight it toward Tier 2 or Pause depending on the financial picture.

Use-of-funds also shapes the funder fit. Inventory, equipment, or hiring maps to funders who prefer productive-use files. Paying off debt or covering a shortfall is a higher-risk profile. Note it in the CRM record.

**Tactic 4: Prior-advance payoff timing as the re-engagement trigger (on re-engagement calls and UCC leads)**

On any call with a merchant known to have an existing or recently repaid advance:
> "When is your current advance scheduled to be paid off, do you know the remaining balance or daily payment?"

> "Your last advance with [funder name from UCC or CRM] was funded about [X] months ago. Is that one fully repaid, or still running?"

A merchant 60-90 days into a 6-month term is often in the prime renewal window. A merchant whose advance was repaid 30-60 days ago (Opportunity 1 trigger) is the highest-intent re-engagement call. Both answers set the urgency framing and the funder-submission strategy without the merchant feeling prospected.

**Tactic 5: Close discovery into the document ask (minute 10-12), bridge to Opportunity 3**

If the merchant clears Tier 1 or strong Tier 2, do not end the call without a live document commitment. This is the bridge between discovery and the document collection engine (Opportunity 3).

Script:
> "Based on what you have told me, I think we can get you an offer. Approval depends on underwriting of your bank statements, that is how every funder makes the decision. To get you an offer within 24-48 hours I need 3 months of business bank statements and a one-page application. Can you send those today?"

If yes: send the request while the merchant is still on the line (or within 10 minutes). Set a 24-hour hard follow-up. A verbal yes that does not produce documents within 48 hours is not a Tier 1 file.

If hesitation: "What is the hold-up, is it finding the statements, or do you want to think about it?" Surface the real objection before leaving the call. "I want to think about it" after a clean financial intake usually means a competing broker is involved or urgency is lower than stated.

---

**What to avoid**

- Asking use-of-funds before the financial intake. It creates rapport but wastes time on an ineligible merchant.
- Softening the stacking and NSF questions with apologies ("I have to ask..."). State them flatly. Apologetic framing signals the operator is uncomfortable with disqualification.
- Accepting "I'm not sure" on the deposit number and moving on. "Not sure" is a data point. Ask for the range, then flag the inconsistency.
- Spending more than 2 minutes on the pitch before documents are requested. This is a transactional call. The merchant does not need a product tour; they need an offer. An offer requires a file.
- Booking a follow-up call as the next step for a Tier 1 merchant. The next step is documents. A second call without documents is a delay, not a pipeline stage.

---

**Over-built or cut**

The plan's "tailored pitch (6 minutes)" block from the generic discovery framework is unnecessary here. MCA is not a consultative product with competing features; it is a fast binary: you qualify and fund, or you do not. The entire call structure should compress to: screen, tier, document ask. The 30-minute discovery format from the generic persona applies to enterprise SaaS, not MCA. Target 12-15 minutes total for a Tier 1 call, not 30.

The Sandler Level 3 "personal/emotional stakes" block is also cut. It is valuable in solution sales; in MCA it reads as pressure and can invite manipulation concerns. The urgency is already in the merchant's situation. Surface it with Q5 (urgency question) and stop.

---

**Top risks**

1. Operator softens disqualification to preserve call volume. A Pause merchant sent to underwriting produces a declined file, a wasted submit, and potential relationship damage with the funder. Set a hard rule: no application taken on a two-stack or sub-$10k merchant, regardless of how warm the call felt.

2. The document ask is deferred to a follow-up call or email. Every hour between verbal yes and statement receipt is a window for a competing broker to capture the file. The document request must happen before the call ends or within 10 minutes after.

3. ADB and NSF answers are taken at face value and not reconciled against statements. Merchants routinely understate negatives and overstate ADB. Build a CRM field for "stated ADB" and "statement ADB" so underwriting gaps are visible and the operator learns to calibrate merchant accuracy over time.

---

**Cross-lane flags**

- Opportunity 2 (qualification page): the 5-question financial intake above should be the exact question set on the page. Page answers should pre-populate the CRM brief so the SDR does not re-ask questions the merchant already answered. Flag to the warm-lead engine lane.
- Opportunity 3 (document collection engine): the Tactic 5 script above is the trigger for the post-call document request draft. The document engine should fire automatically when the SDR marks a call outcome as "Tier 1, doc request sent." Flag to the loan-officer / document-engine lane.
- Opportunity 5 (profitability and clawback ranking): Tier 1 criteria above (18+ months, $25k+, no stack, zero NSF) are the exact fields that should populate the clawback-survivability score in Supabase. Flag to the CRM and ranking lane.
- Compliance: the upfront contract script and the "approval depends on underwriting" close language in Tactic 5 are the compliance guardrails for the call. These should be baked into the lead brief template, not left to operator memory. Flag to the compliance and language lane.

---

**Facts to verify**

- Funder-specific stacking tolerance: some funders will look at a first-position advance if the merchant is 70%+ paid down; others reject any stack. The Tier 2 definition above uses 50% ADB as the threshold, but actual funder guidelines should override this. Confirm with the brokerage's top 3 funders.
- Clawback window by funder: the Tier 1 urgency and use-of-funds flags above are designed to filter out merchants likely to default inside the clawback window, but the window length (60 vs 90 days) varies by funder and ISO agreement. Verify before encoding into the CRM survivability score.
- NSF threshold: "zero or one isolated" is the pass criterion above. Verify whether any funder will approve a file with two NSFs in 90 days if deposits and ADB are strong. Some will; most will not. Update the disqualify threshold accordingly.
