# Pass 2 Audit: Sales Outreach Lane

**Lane:** Cold-to-warm cadences, opener copy, multi-touch sequences, channel mix, and TCPA/DNC compliance for the 90-day funded-deal run.

---

## Strategy

Both high-value cold pools (old funded book and UCC near-payoff contacts) are already warm by signal, not truly cold: prior funding is explicit intent, and a UCC-1 filed 60-150 days ago proves the merchant qualified once and is likely approaching payoff. The entire outreach job is to surface the timing hook fast, survive long enough to book a call, and route the contact to the qualification page for consent capture before any substantive pitch. Sequences are short (7-10 days for re-engagement, 14 days for UCC cold) because the signal has a shelf life: a merchant who just paid off is re-fundable now, not in six weeks. Channel mix is phone-first with voicemail, then email; SMS is consent-only and used only after page opt-in. All copy avoids hype language. The single CTA is always the qualification page link or a direct callback number.

---

## Tactics

**1. Re-engagement cadence for prior funded/repaid book (Opportunity 1)**

Target: any merchant funded 4-18 months ago, not in active clawback, not in default. Pull from company CRM first; these cost nothing.

Sequence length: 5 touches over 8 days. Stop earlier on any reply.

| Day | Channel | Action |
|-----|---------|--------|
| 1 | Phone + voicemail | Live call attempt; leave voicemail if no answer |
| 2 | Email | Opener email (see copy below) |
| 4 | Phone | Second call attempt; no second voicemail |
| 6 | Email | Value-add follow-up with payback calculator link |
| 8 | Email | Soft close / break-up |

Voicemail (30 seconds max):
> "Hi [First Name], this is [Name] from [Brokerage]. You worked with us back in [Month/Year] and your advance closed out cleanly. Given where rates are sitting right now for repeat clients, I wanted to reach out before your renewal window closes. I will send you a short email in the next few minutes. If you want to skip the email, call me back at [number]. No pitch, just a quick check-in."

Email opener (Day 2, under 120 words):
> Subject: Your [Brokerage] file from [Month Year]
>
> [First Name],
>
> You used working capital through us in [Month Year] and paid it down without issues. Repeat clients with clean files typically see better terms on a second round because the repayment history is already on file with our funders.
>
> I am not going to guess at your current situation. If timing is right, a two-minute form on our side would tell you what you qualify for today and what the total payback looks like before you talk to anyone.
>
> [Qualification page link]
>
> If you would rather just talk: [direct phone].
>
> [Name]

Day 8 break-up email:
> Subject: Closing your file
>
> [First Name], I will stop reaching out. If working capital becomes relevant in the next 6-12 months, the link below still works and takes about 90 seconds. No follow-up unless you initiate.
>
> [Qualification page link]

**2. UCC near-payoff cold cadence (Opportunity 4)**

Target: UCC-1 filings from known MCA funders (e.g., Libertas, Kapitus, Cloudfund, WebBank/OnDeck, Yellowstone), filed 60-150 days ago, in TX, FL, NY, CA, or any state where broker registration is confirmed clear. Pull weekly from a UCC vendor at sub-100 USD/month (UCLEGAL, SearchSystems, or state SOS bulk download).

Sequence length: 5 touches over 14 days. The window is real; do not stretch it to 30 days.

| Day | Channel | Action |
|-----|---------|--------|
| 1 | Phone + voicemail | Live call; voicemail on miss |
| 3 | Email | Cold opener |
| 7 | Phone | Second call; no second voicemail |
| 10 | Email | Follow-up with calculator link |
| 14 | Email | Break-up |

Voicemail (30 seconds max):
> "Hi [First Name], this is [Name] from [Brokerage]. I am calling because [Funder Name] filed a lien on your business roughly [X] weeks ago, which usually means you are coming up on the end of your current advance. I wanted to get to you before you get hit with a stack offer from someone else. Short email coming right now. Call me back at [number] if you want to skip straight to a conversation."

Email opener (Day 3, under 130 words):
> Subject: Your [Funder Name] advance - near payoff timing
>
> [First Name],
>
> A public UCC filing shows [Funder Name] funded your business around [approximate month]. Most advances in that range pay off between 3 and 6 months after origination, which puts you in the renewal window now.
>
> I am not going to tell you our rates are better until I see your file. What I can tell you is that repeat funders who see a clean payoff on record will typically offer a higher amount or a lower factor on the next round versus cold-start terms.
>
> Two-minute form, no commitment, shows you what the total payback would look like before you speak with anyone:
>
> [Qualification page link]
>
> [Name] | [Direct phone]

Do NOT reference a specific balance owed or imply knowledge of repayment terms you do not have. State only what the public record shows.

**3. Consent gate before any SMS (TCPA compliance)**

SMS is the highest-reply channel AND the highest-risk channel post the January 2025 FCC one-to-one consent rule. Treat it as follows:

- No SMS to any contact who has not opted in via the qualification page checkbox (explicit, named consent to receive texts from this brokerage specifically).
- Once consent is captured, a single SMS after a no-answer phone attempt is permitted: "Hi [First Name], [Name] from [Brokerage]. Tried calling about your funding renewal. Link to check your options (no commitment): [page link]. Reply STOP to opt out."
- That SMS is one touch in the sequence, not a parallel sequence.
- Never send SMS to UCC-cold contacts until they have completed the qualification page.

**4. Qualification page as the single CTA across all outreach**

Every cadence has one CTA: the qualification page. Not a calendar link, not a phone number, not a PDF. Warm contacts (prior book) may be offered a direct callback number as a second option, but the page comes first because it captures consent and pre-qualifies before the call.

The page link carries UTM parameters by source (re-engagement vs. UCC cold) so warmth scoring knows which signal generated the lead.

**5. Call queue discipline: re-engagement first, UCC cold second, everything else parked**

During the US call window (10:00-19:00 ET), work the call queue in strict order: prior funded book with a live callback or booked call, then prior funded book day-1 new touches, then UCC near-payoff day-1 new touches. Do not start UCC cold until the re-engagement queue is empty for the day. This is not optional; re-engagement reply rates are materially higher and funded faster.

## What to avoid

- **SMS to any contact without page opt-in.** TCPA one-to-one exposure is real regardless of B2B status; the FCC rule does not carve out small businesses cleanly. One complaint to a plaintiff's attorney costs more than 90 days of commission.
- **Voicemail on every attempt.** One voicemail per contact per cadence. Double voicemails train people to block the number.
- **Referencing specific dollar balances or terms from the UCC filing.** You do not have that data; implying you do is a misrepresentation. Reference only what is public: funder name, approximate filing date.
- **Sequences longer than 14 days for either pool.** The signal has a shelf life. A merchant who paid off three months ago and ignored five touches is not a funded deal in this 90-day window; move them to a 90-day re-ping and free the slot.
- **LinkedIn outreach in this window.** Cross-lane: parked per market-brief. Do not spend the operator's limited US-hours time on it.
- **"Guaranteed approval," "cheap money," "lowest rates."** Compliance guardrails per plan.md; also credibility killers in a distrustful market.
- **Calling outside 8:00 AM to 9:00 PM local business time.** TCPA and most state analogs require this; the Israel time zone makes this easy to violate accidentally. Build a hard call-window check into the dashboard.

---

## Over-built or cut

- **Aged-lead vendor spend.** The prior funded book and UCC filings produce better-quality contacts at lower or zero marginal cost. Aged leads are recycled to 5-10 brokers and carry 30-40 percent default per the market-brief conflict note. Cut from budget allocation entirely until months 2-3 if UCC and re-engagement run dry.
- **Multi-channel LinkedIn cadence for cold.** The standard 7-touch LinkedIn-plus-email cadence from the Sales Outreach persona is not appropriate here; LinkedIn is parked. Phone-voicemail-email is the compliant and feasible channel mix for this operator.
- **Breakup email as a separate campaign.** The break-up touch is already built into each cadence above (Day 8 and Day 14). No separate campaign needed.

---

## Top risks

1. **TCPA SMS enforcement.** The January 2025 one-to-one consent rule creates specific written consent requirements that are not satisfied by a generic opt-in. If the qualification page consent language is not correctly scoped to this specific brokerage and SMS channel, the entire SMS program is non-compliant. This is a build dependency: the qualification page consent copy must be reviewed against the rule before any SMS is sent. Flag to compliance/legal lane.

2. **Call window miscalculation from Israel.** Israel Standard Time is UTC+2 (UTC+3 during DST); US Eastern is UTC-4 to UTC-5. A miscalculation of 1 hour puts calls outside the legal window. The dashboard must enforce the call window automatically and flag any lead where the business's state creates an earlier local cutoff (e.g., Hawaii, Alaska).

3. **UCC data staleness and wrong contact.** A UCC filing names a debtor business, not always the decision maker's direct line. If the enrichment step (finding the owner's phone and email) is done with low-quality data, reply rates collapse and the UCC advantage disappears. Budget a portion of the sub-100 USD UCC allocation to a lookup tool (Apollo free tier, Hunter, or ZoomInfo Lite) to get the direct owner contact, not the registered agent.

---

## Cross-lane flags

- **Qualification page consent language (compliance/legal lane):** The SMS consent checkbox copy must be reviewed for TCPA one-to-one specificity before launch. The outreach cadence is blocked on this.
- **Call queue ranking logic (pipeline/systems lane):** Tactic 5 requires the dashboard to surface prior funded book above UCC cold each day. This needs a "lead source type" field and a sort rule in the Supabase schema, not just a manual habit.
- **State broker registration status (compliance lane):** Outreach to UCC contacts in VA, TX (HB 700, OCCC registration due by Dec 31 2026), NY, and CA cannot begin until registration/exemption status is confirmed for each state. The UCC pull should be filtered by state until this is cleared.
- **UTM-to-warmth-score pipeline (systems/warm-engine lane):** Tactic 4 requires the qualification page to pass UTM source into the warmth score. This is a systems build item, not an outreach item.

## Facts to verify

- Whether the January 2025 FCC TCPA one-to-one consent rule applies identically to B2B (small business owner) texting or whether a B2B carveout exists; the rule is under court challenge but currently in effect.
- Whether referencing a UCC funder name and filing date in cold outreach constitutes a regulated communication under any state commercial solicitation law (TX, NY, CA, FL in particular).
- Specific known MCA funder names that appear on UCC-1 filings most frequently in TX, FL, NY, CA so the UCC filter can be narrowed to high-confidence signals rather than all financial UCCs.
- UCC vendor options under 100 USD/month that provide bulk export with debtor contact info (name, address, sometimes phone): UCLEGAL, state SOS bulk download programs, SearchSystems pricing for 2026.
- Whether the plan's voicemail-drop approach (manually leaving a voicemail versus a ringless voicemail drop service) triggers TCPA differently; ringless voicemail drops are treated as calls under current FCC guidance and require the same consent analysis.
